Abstraction
A general idea not based on any particular real person, thing or situation; the quality of being abstract;
The state of thinking deeply about something and not paying attention to what is around you;
The action of removing something from something else; the process of being removed from something else.
Encapsulation
The act of expressing the most important parts of something in a few words, a small space or a single object.
Inheritance
The money, property, etc. that you receive from somebody when they die; the fact of receiving something when somebody dies;
Something from the past or from your family that affects the way you behave, look, etc.
Polymorphism
The fact that something such as an animal or organism can exist in different forms;
A genetic variation.
